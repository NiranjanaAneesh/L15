Sense Organs

Sense organs are specialized body parts that allow humans and other animals to sense the world around them: Eyes: Provide the sense of sight Ears: Provide the sense of hearing Nose: Provides the sense of smell Tongue: Provides the sense of taste Skin: Provides the sense of touch Sense organs contain receptors that relay information to the nervous system. When external stimuli are detected, the sensory organs send signals to the brain through sensory nerves. The brain interprets the signals and responds, helping the sense organs to reciprocate. This process helps humans connect to their environment.

Emotions

Emotions are conscious mental reactions that are experienced as strong feelings. They are a complex reaction pattern that involves: Subjective experience: How the emotion is experienced Physiological response: How the body responds to the emotion Behavioral response: How the emotion is expressed Emotions are a way for people to deal with situations or matters that they find personally significant. The specific quality of the emotion is determined by the significance of the event. For example, if the event involves a threat, fear is likely to be generated.
